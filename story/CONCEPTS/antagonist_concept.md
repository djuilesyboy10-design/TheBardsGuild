Here is a pitch for a questline titled "The Silent Canticle of Balmora."

The Hook: "The Empty Cribs"
The player arrives in Balmora and notices something subtly wrong. The town is quiet. Too quiet. If you speak to local Dunmer, they seem dazed. If you ask about "children," they look confused: "Children? There are no children in Vvardenfell. There never have been."

The "secret" is that a Daedric influence has not only taken the children but erased the memory of them from the population. Only the player (the Nerevarine, protected by fate) and perhaps a few "madmen" like M’Aiq the Liar or a high-ranking Telvanni wizard can see the empty cradles and small shoes left in the dust.

The Antagonist: The Piper of Ash
The antagonist is a rogue worshipper of Clavicus Vile (the Prince of Bargains) or Sheogorath. He carries a unique artifact: The Resonating Flute of Resdayn.

He isn't playing a catchy tune; he’s playing a tonal frequency—similar to Dwemer Tonal Architecture—that resonates with the "inner music" of the mortal soul. To the adults, it sounds like wind in the ash; to the children, it sounds like a doorway to a world without Blight or House politics.

The Twist: The "Clockwork Garden"
The Piper hasn't killed them. He has led them into a forgotten pocket realm of Oblivion or a sealed Dwemer vault beneath the Odai River.

The horror element comes when the player finds the Piper. He isn't a monster; he’s a broken Breton bard who thought he was "saving" them from the coming Dagoth Ur. He’s trapped the children in a state of transcendental paralysis—they are alive, but they are being used as "living batteries" to power a grand musical machine that keeps the memory-wipe spell active over the island.

Gameplay Mechanics & Rewards
Investigation: You have to use the Detect Enchantment spell to find "sound ripples" throughout Balmora and Suran.

The Choice: 1. Break the Spell: Return the children to a world of suffering, ash, and the Sixth House, but restore the truth. 2. Complete the Song: Take the Piper's place, gaining a permanent +20 to Personality and a unique "Charm" spell, but the children are lost forever.

The Artifact: If you kill the Piper, you get The Hollow Reed. It’s a staff that casts Command Humanoid and Rally in a wide area, but drains your own Willpower as you use it.

Why this fits Morrowind
Meta-Commentary: It explains why there are no child NPCs in the game files (the "Memory Wipe").

Tonal Magic: It connects to the lore of Kagrenac and the Dwemer using sound to alter reality.

The Atmosphere: It moves away from "high fantasy" and into "existential dread," which is the heart of the Dunmer experience.
